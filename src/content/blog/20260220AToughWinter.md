we had a tough winter, We had record breaking snow, we had record breaking cold, and record breaking finger nail biting on my part

February this year saw our lowest temp that I can remember at a Wisconsin feeling 9 degrees fairenheit. The coldest last year was 12 and we all complained about how bitter that was. In that same month, we saw 10 inch of snow in a single day and two weeks of snow on the ground

Needless to say, our hives were hit hard. We lost 4 of the 9 that we went into the winter with. On close examination, the hives lost appeared to be really well stocked with honey and pollen. And looking through my notes from the past year, I found that the hives lost were all ones who swarmed late in the season in August, September, and October when I was woefully too busy to manage the hives as needed. Perhaps it was these late swarms that did not allow for the queen to properly mate and not raise enough brood to prepare for the winter. I found no dead bees in my hives so it is possible that the queen died and the rest of the bees drifted into another.

A hard winter, new queens, and a tough move from two bee yards into a brand new one seem to have spelled disaster for our hives. I intend to keep much better notes this year with regards to our hives!
