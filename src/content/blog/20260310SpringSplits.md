Spring has sprung, our cherries and red maples are blooming! A sure sign of good nectar sources in our area. The hives have drones which means that they are starting to think about splitting.

I need to come up with a position description of the hives, so my thought is to describe the hives by their position from the entry gate number the rows from 1-3 starting at the gate and the from right to left on the row as that would indicate their relative proximity to the gate. In total, we have three rows, 3 hives can fit on row 1 and row 3 and two hives can fit on row 2

So start your engines, we started the bee year with a bang!

It was 84 degrees outside when we stared with our inspections, unseasonably warm for the month

Our first hive (position r1h1):
A quick inspection found our queen in the upper brood box, with 10 frames of brood, resources, and honey. It was a classic split into two hives, putting the new in the position of r2h2.

Second hive (r1h2)
Classic split and second hive places in r1h3

Third hive (r2h1)
Tiny tiny tiny amount of eggs and brood, busted down to one box. Don't think they will make it

Fourth hive (r3h1)
Emergency demaree, I could not find the queen, so I did the next best think. I shook all the bees to the bottom box, put the brood on top and moved on. Not the best, but there won't be a swarm in three days
